Augmentation Ablation Study (Complete)
=======================================

Configuration:
- Seed: 42 (for consistency)
- Augmentation: SpecAugment + Mixup
- Quantization: INT8 (full-integer quantization)
- Test set: voicer28-30 (n=361)

  Delta: -9.41 pp degradation WITHOUT augmentation
  Status: Augmentation CRITICAL for BC-ResNet; model fails without it

Interpretation:
===============

The ablation reveals a complex relationship between SpecAugment+Mixup and model architecture:

1. DS-CNN: Augmentation neutral/slightly harmful (~+1.4pp gain from removal)
   - Despite being the lightest model, DS-CNN performs equivalently with or without augmentation
   - The upstream dataset augmentation (Pass 1/Pass 2 speed & noise) provides sufficient signal

2. Vanilla CNN: Augmentation slightly harmful (~+1.9pp gain from removal)
   - Like DS-CNN, Vanilla CNN benefits from the simpler training recipe without SpecAugment+Mixup
   - Suggests the augmentation pipeline may introduce noise on this small dataset (12 classes, ~10,800 train samples)

3. BC-ResNet: Augmentation ESSENTIAL (+9.4pp degradation from removal)
   - This deeper architecture with residual connections depends critically on augmentation
   - WITHOUT augmentation, BC-ResNet's validation accuracy plateau is much lower
   - Early stopping may trigger earlier due to overfitting on non-augmented small dataset

Recommendation:
===============

CONDITIONAL AUGMENTATION STRATEGY:

Option A (Conservative - Recommended for Paper):
- Use augmentation for BC-ResNet only (architectures that benefit from regularization)
- Remove augmentation for Vanilla CNN and DS-CNN (lighter models that don't need it)
- Provides best per-architecture performance

Option B (Uniform - Current Implementation):
- Keep augmentation across all models for consistency
- Slight performance penalty on Vanilla CNN and DS-CNN (~2pp each)
- Simpler to implement and explain

Option C (Remove Entirely - NOT Recommended):
- Remove augmentation across all models
- BC-ResNet would drop to 75.35% (unacceptable 9.4pp loss)

CHOSEN APPROACH FOR PAPER:
Apply augmentation uniformly across all models for pipeline consistency.
Note in paper that SpecAugment+Mixup is "not beneficial for lighter architectures
(DS-CNN, Vanilla CNN) but essential for deeper residual networks (BC-ResNet)."

The small dataset size and heavy upstream augmentation make the additional
SpecAugment+Mixup pipeline redundant for simpler models, but critical regularization
for complex architectures prone to overfitting.
