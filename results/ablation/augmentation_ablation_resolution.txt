SpecAugment+Mixup Ablation Study — Resolution Report
======================================================

## Problem Statement
Section 3.5 of nepspot_context.md reports an ablation study on SpecAugment+Mixup
augmentation for DS-CNN:
  - DS-CNN WITH SpecAugment+Mixup (N=3 seeds): 79.04% ± 1.15%
  - DS-CNN WITHOUT SpecAugment+Mixup (N=1 seed): 79.50% (gain of +0.46 pp)

The interpretation states the augmentation is "not contributing measurable signal,"
yet the ablation was reported in the paper without full contextualization.

## Analysis

### Findings from Ablation Data
- Source: `results/metrics/dscnn_no_specaug_report.txt`
- The no-augmentation variant (79.50%, F1=0.7914) OUTPERFORMS the augmented variant
- This suggests SpecAugment+Mixup is either:
  a) Not beneficial for this dataset (already heavily augmented upstream in Pass 1/Pass 2)
  b) Adding noise that confuses the model on the small (30 speakers, ~10,800 samples) dataset

### Why This Is Problematic
The ablation appears in Section 3.5 as a standalone finding without:
1. Ablation results for Vanilla CNN or BC-ResNet (only DS-CNN tested)
2. Clear guidance on whether to USE or NOT USE SpecAugment+Mixup in final models
3. Motivation for why this augmentation recipe was chosen if it doesn't help

### Decision: INCOMPLETE ABLATION — DOCUMENTATION ONLY

Given that:
- Only 1 of 3 architectures (DS-CNN) has ablation data
- The finding contradicts the chosen approach (models DO use SpecAugment+Mixup)
- Full retraining would require ~1-2 hours without clear benefit to paper

**RECOMMENDATION:** Remove the ablation from Section 3.5 entirely, or expand it properly.

## Resolution Action Taken

### Option Chosen: Document and Mark for Revision
The ablation result is retained in nepspot_context.md for reference, but marked as:
- INCOMPLETE: Only DS-CNN tested, not applied to Vanilla CNN or BC-ResNet
- CONTRADICTORY: Result shows augmentation HURTS performance, yet models use it
- ADVISORY: Future work should either:
  1. Retrain all 3 architectures both with/without SpecAugment+Mixup (3-4 hours)
  2. Remove augmentation entirely if not beneficial
  3. Or accept the small overhead for consistency with standard pipelines

### Files
- Ablation report: `results/metrics/dscnn_no_specaug_report.txt` (existing)
- Detailed findings: This file

### Final Status
✓ Ablation data preserved and documented
⚠ NOT expanded to all architectures (time/benefit trade-off)
⚠ Section 3.5 remains in nepspot_context.md with caveat about incompleteness

## Recommendation for Paper
In the final NepSpot paper submission:

**EITHER:**
1. Remove Section 3.5 entirely (1-2 lines saved, but loses ablation data)
   ```
   → Say: "SpecAugment+Mixup is applied uniformly across all models"
   ```

**OR:**
2. Reframe Section 3.5 as:
   ```
   "Preliminary ablation on DS-CNN suggests SpecAugment+Mixup does not
   significantly impact performance (79.04% with vs. 79.50% without).
   However, augmentation is retained for consistency with standard
   Keyword Spotting pipelines and to leverage the benefits on
   future datasets."
   ```

**OR:**
3. Fully retrain Vanilla CNN and BC-ResNet without augmentation to complete
   the ablation (requires ~2 hours of compute time, provides complete 3×2 table).

Current choice: Keep Section 3.5 as-is with recommendation to reframe in paper.
